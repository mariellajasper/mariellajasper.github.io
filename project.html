Project: Unpaid Work, Lifestyle, and Gender

How do unpaid care and domestic work shape the lives of men and women?

Aims of project

Unpaid work is often unmeasured and undervalued. The prevailing measure of ‘economic health’ is gross domestic product (GDP), a monetary value of goods and services produced in an economy.

Yet GDP does not include unpaid care and domestic work, for which data is lacking, meaning the impact of unpaid work on lifestyle, wellbeing, and economic opportunities often goes unrecognised. For example, in 2016, the value of the UK’s unpaid household service work was estimated at 63.1% of GDP (ONS, 2018), but comparable statistics covering multiple years and countries are rare.

In a world with aging populations, artificial intelligence, and pervasive economic and gender inequality, valuing and analysing unpaid work is imperative. This project aims quantify and visualise how unpaid care and domestic work affect lifestyle and time use, wellbeing, and economic opportunities, and analyse how these impacts vary for men and women.
